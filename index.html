About Me

My name is Anthony Wong and I am in my graduating semester at Georgia Tech where I am pursuing a BS in Computer Science with a concentration in Intelligence and Information Internetworks. I've also been accepted into the Master's program at Georgia Tech in Computer Science with a concentration in Machine Learning, which I plan to start in Spring 2024 and finish Fall 2024.

I've additionally been lucky enough to secure a number of profession internship opportunities spanning different industries. Following my first year are Georgia Tech, I worked at Georgia Tech Research Institute as an Electronic Systems Integration Intern. The next summer, I worked at General Motors as a Big Data Analytics Intern. This past summer, I worked at Salesforce as a Software Engineer Intern, and I am returning to this role in the upcoming summer for the same team. As such, I've been able to develop my technical skills all while gaining insight into how Computer Science is used in different facets in different industries. Though I love software engineering, I hope to move into a Machine Learning Engineer Role in the future as it has remained a deep passion of mine throughout my academic career.

Projects

Regional Socioeconomic Prediction Data Viz (Big Data Big Impact at Georgia Tech)


Poverty levels around the world influence how government and charitable organizations allocate resources, construct policies, and conduct research. This long-running project seeks to accurately predict development levels of geographic regions based on satellite imagery. The data visualization side of this project specifically focuses on constructing an interactable user interface that allows users to explore different regions in different countries and see the data as predicted from the ML models. Click here to check out the repo.

Tower Defense Game (CS2340 Project)


CS2340 Objects and Design deals with large OOP programs with a focus on quality processes, effective debugging techniques, and testing to assure a quality product. A large part of the course was a semester-long group project, usually a game written in JavaFX. For this iteration of the course, we were instructed to create a tower defense game, where the player must defend their own base from incoming waves of enemies by placing damage-dealing towers. Our design aimed to build upon the popular concept of elements: earth, water, fire, and air. Click here to check out the repo.

Mini City Analysis (Big Data Big Impact at Georgia Tech)


Mini City is an organization in Atlanta that uses software to unlock legal forms of identification for the homeless and homeless care providers. The analysis side of the project focused on the application of K-means, Principal Component Analysis, t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding, and Neural Networks to draw conclusions from the client database provided by the organization. This project has since been discontinued.

SALON - An Intelligent Debate Platform (HackGT 7 - Emerging Track Best Web Dev Award)


SALON is an application that seeks to bring the value of civil discussion to a world that often lacks it. It is a platform that not only allows for telephone-style debates, but also transcribes and analyzes the conversation, creates transcribed text posts, and cultivates a space for meaningful, productive discussion. Learn more.

Poker Simulation and Data Representation (Personal Project)


Back in high school, I was really into poker. Wanting to get better at the game, I wanted to simulate the game and collect data on the hands that won the most and with what final hand. The logic of the game was built from scratch and data was collected compiled into graphs. Please excuse the poor quality of the code. High school me wasn't very good. Click here to check out the repo.

English Premier League Score Prediction (Personal Project)


As I've stated before, soccer has been and always be a huge part of my life. In high school, I stumbled upon a massive database documenting nearly 20 years of matches played in the English Premier League. One section of the statistics collected was the halftime stats of the game. I then used this data, along with some multiple linear regression to predict the final score. I then extended this to allow the user to input match data at halftime and predict the final score.

Documents

LEENet: Learned Early Exit Network

Learning Optimal Early Exit Policy for Efficiency Improvements in DNNs

The below pdf and slideshow documents our project proposal and tentative results/analysis for our novel architecture for DNNs which seeks to utilize the concept of early exits to improve upon the efficiencies of various existing networks. We plan to eventually publish when we have the requisite work completed.

Exploring Denoising Autoencoders and Analyzing Their Efficacy for Data Clustering on the MNIST Fashion Dataset

The below pdf documents my exploration on using denoising autoencoders with noisy images to produce latent embeddings that could be used to KMeans cluster MNIST-Fashion Images in clusters representative of their target classes. These results were then compared to an autoencoder with images that were not noisy to see the impact of noise on clustering despite trained denoising autoencoders. This submission was deemed the best of the class by Dr. Ling Liu
